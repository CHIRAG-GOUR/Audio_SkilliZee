1 – Introduction to Storytelling Basics

1.1 – What is a Book? Categories & Story Structure

A book is a collection of pages bound together, containing words and sometimes images that tell a story or provide information. Categories include fiction (made-up stories like adventure or mystery) and non-fiction (true information like biographies or science books). Story structure typically has a beginning (introducing characters and setting), middle (where the action happens), and end (resolution of the plot).

1.2 – What is a publisher? Who is an author?

An author is the person who writes the book, creating the stories or ideas. A publisher is a company or person who helps print, distribute, and sell the book to readers, making sure it reaches bookstores or online platforms.

1.3 – Structure Of a Story – Keep It Basic

The basic structure of a story includes the introduction (setting the scene), rising action (building tension), climax (the peak moment), falling action (wrapping up events), and resolution (how it all ends).

1.4 – What is a cover page? Key aspects of a foreword

The cover page is the front of the book, with the title, author's name, and an eye-catching image to attract readers. A foreword is an introduction written by someone else, explaining why the book is important or sharing insights about the author/story.

1.5 – Plot, Plot points, characters, theme, tone, Writing styles

The plot is the sequence of events in the story. Plot points are key moments that change the direction. Characters are the people or animals in the story. Theme is the main idea or message. Tone is the mood (happy, scary). Writing styles vary, like descriptive (detailed scenes) or dialogue-heavy.

2 – Book Writing Made Easy With AI

2.1 – Science behind using a prompt

A prompt is a question or instruction given to AI to generate text. The science involves machine learning where AI predicts words based on patterns from vast data, making writing faster and easier.

2.2 – Creating Characters and Plot with Help of AI

Use AI to brainstorm characters (names, traits, backstories) and plots (events, twists) by giving prompts like "Create a brave hero for an adventure story."

2.3 – Using CANVA

Canva is a free tool for designing book covers and illustrations. Drag and drop elements, add text, and choose templates to make your book visually appealing.

2.4 – Publishing and Sharing Your Book

Once written, use platforms like Amazon KDP or Google Docs to publish. Share via email, social media, or print copies for friends and family.

3 – Final Capstone Project

3.1 – Project

3.1 – Project Submission
